  1. Download Adium from http://adium.im/

    Open the .dmg and drag Adium into your Applications folder.

    Open the Applications folder, and launch Adium from there.

    (Adium may ask for access to your contacts – this can be handy, but it's fine to click Don't Allow if you prefer.)

  2. The first time you open Adium you'll see a Setup Assistant. We're going to assume you closed it, but you can Continue through if you prefer.

  3. Instead, open the Preferences panel either by pressing ⌘, or by selecting it from the Adium menu.

  4. Click the + button in the lower left corner of the Accounts pane in the Preferences window, and select XMPP.

    (XMPP is the new name for Jabber, and they are used interchangeably.)

  5. Enter {{ user }}@{{ domain }} as your username, enter the password you chose when you signed up for Dashdash, and then click OK.

    WHATEVER YOU DO, do not click the Register New Account button – it's a trap! Click OK instead.

  6. Your Dashdash account will connect automatically, and you can close the Preferences window.

    Then find your Contacts List window, which should already be open.

  7. Look for the Dashdash Contacts and Dashdash Conversations buddy list groups.

    Adium hides empty contact groups by default, so if they're not there it's only because no one is online or chatting. You can customize what Adium hides from the View menu.

    If you're lucky, the first time you sign in a friendly chat bot named @{{ helpbot }} will say hello.

    or

  8. For an overview of the basic features, watch this screencast .gif. You can also watch this slightly out-of-date screencast video.

    Feel free to email us, or ping @lehrblogger if you need help.

  9. Adium-only pro tip: you can get a Growl notification whenever there's a new conversations that you can see!

    First, right click on the group header bar for Dashdash Conversations in your contact list.

    Then, select the third tab (with the alarm bell icon), and double-click on the event titled Contact is seen.

    Select the notification type you want, and you're done!

  1. Messages, which used to be called iChat, comes with OS X and should already be installed on your computer.

    Look in your Applications folder for something that looks like this

  2. If this is the first time you've used Messages, you'll be prompted to set up iMessage.

    It's fine to Skip that for now, and it will send you next to the account creation screen.

  3. If you've used Messages before, then open the Preferences panel either by pressing ⌘, or by selecting it from the Messages menu.

  4. Select the Accounts tab, and then click the + button in the lower left corner of the window.

  5. Select Jabber as the Account Type from the dropdown list.

    (Jabber is the old name for XMPP, and they are used interchangeably.)

  6. Enter {{ user }}@{{ domain }} as your username, enter the password you chose when you signed up for Dashdash, and then click Sign In.

    (You can leave the Server Options as their defaults.)

  7. You might get a Non-secure Jabber Login error message, and you can just click Continue.

    (I think this happens because the password itself is not separately encrypted, but all the connections use SSL so it should be sufficiently secure.)

  8. Now that you're signed in, look for a second Messages window that says Buddies at the top.

    If you don't see it, open it either by pressing ⌘1 or by selecting it from the Window menu.

  9. Look for the Dashdash Contacts and Dashdash Conversations buddy list groups.

    Messages hides empty buddy groups, so if they're not there it's only because no one is online or chatting. Don't worry and check back later!

    If you're lucky, the first time you sign in a friendly chat bot named @{{ helpbot }} will say hello.
