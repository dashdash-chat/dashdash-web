welcome to Dashdash!

Dashdash is similar to chat services like Gchat and AIM, but manages your contact list for you:

  1. People you've talked with before, that signed up using the same invitation, or that you follow on Twitter will automatically appear in your Dashdash Contacts group in your contact list.
  2. If you and a friend both appear in each other's contact list, then their active conversations will appear in your Dashdash Conversations group. You can join these conversations by sending a message to them, just like you might walk up to a group of friends in real life.

Dashdash works with existing XMPP/Jabber chat client apps – pick one below to get started!

    {% if client == 'adium' %}

  1. If you have a Mac, download Adium from http://adium.im/.

    Open the .dmg and drag Adium into your Applications folder.

    Open the Applications folder, and launch Adium from there.

    (Adium may ask for access to your contacts – this can be handy, but it's fine to click Don't Allow if you prefer.)

  2. The first time you open Adium you'll see a Setup Assistant. We're going to assume you closed it, but you can Continue through if you prefer.

  3. Instead, open the Preferences panel either by pressing ⌘, or by selecting it from the Adium menu.

  4. Click the + button in the lower left corner of the Accounts pane in the Preferences window, and select XMPP.

    (XMPP is the new name for Jabber, and they are used interchangeably.)

  5. Enter {{ user }}@{{ domain }} as your username, enter the password you chose when you signed up for Dashdash, and then click OK.

    WHATEVER YOU DO, do not click the Register New Account button – it's a trap! Click OK instead.

  6. Your Dashdash account will connect automatically, and you can close the Preferences window.

    Then find your Contacts List window, which should already be open.

  7. Look for the Dashdash Contacts and Dashdash Conversations buddy list groups.

    Adium hides empty contact groups by default, so if they're not there it's only because no one is online or chatting. You can customize what Adium hides from the View menu.

    If you're lucky, the first time you sign in a friendly chat bot named @{{ helpbot }} will say hello. Otherwise, send someone a message!

  8. Adium-only pro tip: you can get a Growl notification whenever there's a new conversations that you can see!

    First, right click on the group header bar for Dashdash Conversations in your contact list.

    Then, select the third tab (with the alarm bell icon), and double-click on the event titled Contact is seen.

    Select the notification type you want, and you're done!

    Look in your Applications folder for something that looks like this

    18. {% elif client == 'pidgin' %}

  18. If you use Windows, download Pidgin from http://pidgin.im/.

    Find the installer in your Downloads folder (or wherever you decided to save it), and launch it to start the Setup Wizard .

    (You may need to give Pidgin permission to make changes to your computer. Pidgin is an established open-source project, so if you prefer you can read and compile the source yourself...)

  19. The Setup Wizard defaults will work fine, so keep clicking Next > until the installation is complete.

    After Pidgin is installed, launch the program from the Start Menu.

  20. The first time you launch Pidgin, you'll see the Accounts window. Click Add... to add a new account, but if you accidentally close this window, then...

  21. ... you can always open it again by clicking Accounts -> Manage Accounts in your Buddy List window, or by pressing Ctrl+A.

  22. Select XMPP from the dropdown list as the Protocol for your Dashdash account.

    (XMPP is the new name for Jabber, and they are used interchangeably.)

  23. Enter {{ user }}@{{ domain }} as your username, enter the password you chose when you signed up for Dashdash, and then click Add.

    You can optionally specify a Resource for this connection, and you can leave all of the other settings as their defaults.

    You may also want to have Pidgin Remember password, but do not Create this new account on the server – your Dashdash account was created when you signed up on this website, and now you're just using it to sign in.

  24. Your Dashdash account will connect automatically, and you can Close the Accounts window.

    Then find your Buddy List window again, which should still be open.

  25. Look for the Dashdash Contacts and Dashdash Conversations buddy groups.

    Pidgin hides empty buddy groups by default, so if they're not there it's only because no one is online or chatting. You can see these groups by clicking the Buddies menu and then selecting Show -> Empty Groups.

    If you're lucky, the first time you sign in a friendly chat bot named @{{ helpbot }} will say hello. Otherwise, send someone a message!
